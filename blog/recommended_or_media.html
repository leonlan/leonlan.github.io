Introduction

As a freshly-started PhD Candidate in Operations Research (OR), I have read dozens of books and papers to familiarize myself with the state-of-the-art. The academic literature is obviously one of the best places to find this information, but it's not the only place where you can learn about state-of-the-art OR. Media such as blogs, podcasts and seminars have also been a valuable resource to enhancing my understanding of OR. These outlets present informal discussions - uncommon in the academic literature - and listening to an 1-hour talk of the inventor of the state-of-the-art algorithm in flow shop scheduling is a lot easier than reading his 100+ publications on that same topic.

Below I present my curated list of blogs, podcasts and seminars that are of interests to PhD Candidates in OR.

Blogs

  • Yet Another Math Programming Consultant - Erwin Kalvelagen
  • Punk Rock Operations Research - Laura Albert
  • OR in an OB world - Paul Rubin
  • Farkas Dilemma - Austin Buchanan
  • OR in Devon, UK

For a larger overview of OR blogs, you can check out this list by Michael Trick or this thread on OR StackExchange.
