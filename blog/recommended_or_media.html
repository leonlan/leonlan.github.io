Introduction

As a freshly-started PhD Candidate in Operations Research (OR), I have read dozens of academic books and papers to familiarize myself with the state-of-the-art. The academic literature is obviously one of the best places to find this information, but it's not the only place where you can learn about OR.

The OR community is also active in media outlets such as blogs, podcasts and seminars. These outlets have been a valuable source of information to me, and in many cases, I even learn about things that are near impossible to find in the academic literature.

So, without further ado, let me present my list of blogs, podcasts and seminars that I think are of interest to other PhD Candidates in OR.

Blogs

  • Yet Another Math Programming Consultant - Erwin Kalvelagen
  • Punk Rock Operations Research - Laura Albert
  • OR in an OB world - Paul Rubin
  • Farkas Dilemma - Austin Buchanan
  • OR in Devon, UK

For a larger overview of OR blogs, you can check out this list by Michael Trick or this thread on OR StackExchange.

Podcasts

  • Subject To - Anand Subramanian
  • INFORMS: Resoundingly Human - Ashley Kilgore
  • Optimizing You - Anthony Karahalios

Seminars

  • Scheduling seminar
  • VeRoLog webinar
  • Dutch Seminar on Optimization
  • Discrete Optimization Talks
  • ISyE Seminar Series
  • Machine Learning NeEDS Mathematical Optimization

Other recommendations

Here are some other recommendations that may be of interest, but do not belong to any of the categories above.

  • OR StackExchange
  • Optimization Challenges
  • OR Twitter

That's it! I hope you have found something interesting in my list of OR blogs, podcasts and seminars. Feel free to contact me if you have any other suggestions.
